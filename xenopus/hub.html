Xenopus UCSC hub

Description

The ChIP-sequencing tracks were produced as part of a project funded by NICHD to generate epigenome reference maps of Xenopus tropicalis embryos. Intergenic DNA contains functional genomic elements which are largely unannotated. The biochemical features of chromatin, such as post-translational modifications of histone proteins, determine its accessibility and function. The combination of different histone modifications can also be used to partition the genome in distinct chromatin states, such as promoters, enhancers, transcribed regions, and repressed heterochromatin.

Methods

ChIP-sequencing was performed as described (Akkers et al. 2009, Akkers et al. 2012, Van Heeringen et al. 2014). The genome browser tracks have been generated for the histone modifications H3K4me1, H3K4me3, H3K9ac, H3K9me2, H3K9me3, H3K27me3, H3K36me3 and H4K20me3, in addition to RNA polymerase II and p300. At five stages of development (NF stages 9, 10.5, 12.5, 16 and 30) they have been mapped with or without repetitive reads, to allow assessing histone modfications at repetitive heterochromatic regions as well as unique regions within the genome. In addition they have been mapped to different X.tropicalis genome assemblies.

Using the track data hub

A description of the track hub can be found at the following location: http://veenstra.ncmls.nl/trackhub.htm Please contact Georgios Georgiou (email g.georgiou [@] science.ru.nl) for technical questions.

Credits and Data access policy

The data were generated in the Gert Jan Veenstra lab at Radboud University (The Netherlands). The track hub includes published data but also provides pre-publication access to data. The unpublished data can be freely used on a per gene locus basis. Please contact Gert Jan Veenstra (email g.veenstra [@] science.ru.nl) for all other use.
